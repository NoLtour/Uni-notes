## The Laplacian ($\nabla^{2}$)

### Equation

The Laplacian is an important differentiation operation equivalent to taking the [[divergence operator|divergence]] of a [[del operator|gradient]] which can be applied to a vector or scalar function:

> ### $$\begin{align*} \text{scalar case:}& & \nabla^{2}f  &\equiv \nabla\cdot(\nabla f)  \equiv \text{div:}( \text{grad}f ) = \frac{\delta^{2}}{\delta x^{2}} f + \frac{\delta^{2}}{\delta y^{2}} f+ \frac{\delta^{2}}{\delta z^{2}}f \\\\ \text{vector case:}& & \nabla^{2}\vec{f} &= \hat{i} \nabla^{2} (\vec{f} \cdot\hat{i}) + \hat{j} \nabla^{2} (\vec{f} \cdot\hat{j})+ \hat{k} \nabla^{2} (\vec{f} \cdot\hat{k}) =  \left(\nabla^{2} f_{\hat{i}},\nabla^{2} f_{\hat{j}}, \nabla^{2} f_{\hat{k}}\right) \end{align*}$$
> ### $$\begin{align*} \nabla^{2}  = \frac{\delta^{2}}{\delta x^{2}}   + \frac{\delta^{2}}{\delta y^{2}}  + \frac{\delta^{2}}{\delta z^{2}}  \end{align*}$$
>> where:
>> $\nabla^{2}=$ [[the Laplacian|Laplacian operator]]
>> $\nabla f=$ [[del operator|grad]] of $f$
>> $\nabla \cdot ...=$ [[divergence operator|divergence]] of something
>> $f=$ some scalar function
>> $\vec{f}=\hat{i}f_{\hat{i}} + \hat{j} f_{\hat{j}} + \hat{k} f_{\hat{k}}=$ some vector function
>> $\hat{i},\hat{j},\hat{k}=$ unit vectors in the x,y,z directions
